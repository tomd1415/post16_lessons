Answer guidance (Lesson 1)

This page summarises the handbook’s review question answers and class-discussion guidance in teacher-friendly wording. It is not intended for learners as a “giveaway sheet”.

Review question answers (paraphrased)

QAnswer guidance
1Computing includes processing data / doing calculations.
2Computational thinking is analysing problems and identifying solutions/approaches to solve them.
3“Apprehension” is not a typical CT method.
4Decomposition example: split “design a robot” into smaller design tasks (power, sensors, hand, etc.).
5A program is an algorithm written in a form suitable for a computer to run.
6Least likely: “intuition” (algorithms need clear rules, not gut feelings).
7Common recipe pattern: “set the oven to a temperature” (also highlights variation: different temps/times).
8Most relevant abstraction for robo‑chef cooking: quantities/measurements of ingredients.

Discussion wrap-up points

Computers are strong at…

  • Repetitive tasks
  • Tasks that can be expressed as well-defined, precise steps
  • Working consistently at scale (lots of data, lots of cases)

Computers struggle with…

  • Tasks where “the small steps” are hard to define
  • Ambiguous goals or missing success criteria
  • Open-ended “common sense” judgement (without lots of examples)
Use the sorting activities to make the idea concrete: “sorting” becomes lots of “compare two items” decisions.
