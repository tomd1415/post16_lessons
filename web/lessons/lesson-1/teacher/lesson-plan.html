Lesson plan (adaptable)

This is a suggested flow based on Lesson 1 themes: computational thinking (decomposition, pattern recognition, abstraction, algorithms) and “what is a computer?” discussion.

Learning objectives

    Suggested timing (50–60 minutes)

    TimePhaseWhat to do
    0–5 Starter Quick question: “Name 3 things that might be a computer.” Collect a few answers.
    5–15 Discussion Run Activity 1 as a class: place 4–6 devices, then debate boundaries.
    15–25 Vocabulary Pairs do Activity 2. Quick whole-class recap of the 4 CT methods.
    25–40 Group task Teams do Activity 3 (decomposition tree). Encourage action + sensing + reusable functions.
    40–50 Algorithm Do Activity 6 together. Emphasise “compare two” as the smallest step.
    50–60 Plenary Mini assessment (Activity 8) or a quick reflection: “Where did we use abstraction today?”

    Materials

    • Whiteboards or A2 paper + markers (for the robo‑chef design)
    • Access to a browser for interactive activities (or print from Printable cards)

    Differentiation options

    Support

    • Use teacher mode to reveal hints.
    • Limit device cards to 6 items and focus on “inputs/processing/outputs”.
    • For robo‑chef: give a fixed speciality (e.g., “sandwich maker”) and provide a starter function list.

    Stretch

    • Use the harder “circle” sorting challenge in Activity 6.
    • Add “constraints” to the robo‑chef (cost, safety standards, reliability).
    • Use Big problems to force practical software ideas.
