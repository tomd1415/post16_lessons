Lesson plan

Overview

This lesson progresses from a first look at the Boolean data type in lesson 6 to

examining Boolean logic in more detail. It looks at how Boolean expressions are

built, comparison operators and Boolean operators. Booleans are combined with

variables to produce a guessing game. Finally, parentheses and operator

precedence are considered, producing more complex expressions.

Learning objectives

  • Use Boolean logic expressions in a program
  • Recognise types of Boolean logic expressions to generate a true or false value like: =, >, <, >=, <=, <>, !=, ==, AND, OR, NOT
  • Understand the precedence of operators and the order of evaluation in complex expressions

Exercises in this lesson

  • Logic in Search Terms — Algorithms (from lesson 1)
  • And and Or in Controlling a Washing Machine — Reinforce understanding of OR and AND Boolean
